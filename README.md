## Research Overview

Our study investigates whether standard user modeling assumptions hold for humanities scholars working with digital archives. We identify four dimensions where scholarly information-seeking behavior diverges from commercial modeling assumptions:

1. **Context Volatility** — Preferences shift rapidly with research tasks and roles
2. **Epistemic Trust** — Relevance requires verifiable provenance, not social proof
3. **Contrastive Seeking** — Scholars seek items that challenge, not just confirm
4. **Strand Continuity** — Work spans long-term research threads, not discrete sessions

### Methodology

**Study Design**: Five 60-minute focus groups, each following a consistent semi-structured format.

**Session Structure**:
- Introduction and context (10 minutes)
- Three thematic discussions with scenario-based mockups (45 minutes):
  - Topic 1: Visibility and Representation
  - Topic 2: Adaptation and Access
  - Topic 3: Transparency and Trust
- Conclusion (5 minutes)

**Analysis**: Abductive thematic analysis combining deductive coding from RecSys literature with inductive pattern identification. We specifically sought statements that *conflicted* with standard RecSys assumptions.
